Michael John Gambon

The actor who has stolen everyone's heart

Here's a time Michael Gambon's life:

  • Full Name: Sir Michael John Gambon
  • Date of Birth: October 19, 1940
  • Place of Birth: Dublin, Ireland
  • Early Life:
    • Moved to London with his family at a young age.
    • Trained as an engineer before turning to acting.
    • Studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA).
  • Theatre Career:
    • Began acting in the early 1960s.
    • Gained recognition working with Laurence Olivier at the National Theatre.
    • Starred in numerous acclaimed stage productions, including Shakespearean roles.
    • Won multiple Laurence Olivier Awards for his theatre performances.
  • Film and Television:
    • Rose to wider fame with the 1986 TV series The Singing Detective.
    • Appeared in many films, including Gosford Park, The King's Speech, and Fantastic Mr. Fox.
    • Most famously portrayed Professor Albus Dumbledore in the Harry Potter films (from the third film onward, after Richard Harris's death).
  • Awards and Honors:
    • Knighted in 1998 for services to drama.
    • Recipient of numerous BAFTA Awards and nominations.
    • Highly respected by peers for his versatility and commanding presence.
  • Personal Life:
    • Married to Anne Miller; had three children.
    • Known for his privacy, wit, and love of cars.
  • Later Years and Death:
    • Retired from stage acting in 2015 due to memory issues.
    • Passed away on September 27, 2023, at the age of 82.
